This d3 visualization modified from Mike Bostock enables viewers to zoom in and view trends of each variable at each city. From this, we are able to observe at city level, what the relationships between housing prices and land prices, and between land prices and land sales were like, given its population and salary level.

We observed that in some cities such as Hangzhou, housing price seemed to be promoted by land prices in the previous year, while in other places such as Jiujiang, housing prices peaked first, followed by gradual increase in residential land prices. We also observed that land prices and new land sales were often times negatively related. For example, if we clicked on Beijing, land prices and new land sales were almost mirror images.
