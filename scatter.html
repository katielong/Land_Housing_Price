Land Price and Land Sales

Unlike most other countries, land in China is owned by the government. By controlling the amount of land available for sale each year, the government may be able to gain profit by increasing the transaction price. This is the phenomenon that we are interested in: if there is a negative relationship between land price and land sales. We control for urban population and average salary since these are the main two factors influencing the demand for land sales that were used commonly in past literature. In this animated scatterplot, we highlighted major cities vs. minor cities and observed any different relationships across the two groups.
